Become a part of the DHT Team

If you are a conscientious individual who wants seasonal work and enjoys travel - in an atmosphere of mutual respect for the employer, employee and the customer, we have a job for you!

Job Description

We are looking for candidates who have at least six months farm experience and/or truck driving experience. Also, other related experience or training such as welding, machinery or truck maintenance and repair, and engineering is a plus.

Requirements

  • Individuals must have a clean driving record and a stable work history.
  • All employees are required to apply for a US drivers license upon arrival and own a smart phone.
  • Employees must pass a physical exam and pre-employment drug screen by company selected physician.
  • Operator/drivers are expected to perform required daily maintenance and field repairs when needed.
  • A cooperative attitude and a willingness to work with others in a team environment is a must.
  • Candidates must speak, read, and write English. Consulate interviews are required in your respective countries. Criminal and driving history will be examined.

    • Employee Compensation

    The US Department of Labor sets our minimum wage rates and these rates differ by state and are adjusted periodically. Current rates are available upon request. Employees are paid bi-weekly. We guarantee a minimum salary for the season based on Department of Labor standards and greater potential earnings possible dependent on hours worked, safety, and performance.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What are the dates of employment?

    There are two employment dates you can apply for either one or both, but you can only get hired for one of the work dates.

  • Option 1: First Employment Period - March 15th through January 15th
    • These are the start and end dates for the H2A Visa, but arrival usually starts around April 15th and departure usually occurs around December 15.
  • Option 2: Second Employment Period - July 1st through January
    • These are the start and end dates for this Work Period. The employees will arrive around July 1st and depart around December 15th.
    •  How much will I get paid?

    All employees get paid an hourly rate, which is adjusted every year. First year employees will be paid an hourly rate set by the US Department of Labor (DOL). In 2022, the DOL hourly rates for a sampling of states were as follows:

  • Arizona: $14.79 per hour
  • South Dakota: $16.47 per hour
  • North Dakota: $16.47 per hour

    •  How Many Hours will I work?

    This depends on the weather but if the sun is shining and the crops are ready, you will work upwards of 80 hours a week, 6 or 7 days a week, 12-14 hours a day. You will be required to frequently submit your work hours (and other agricultural/harvest relevant job data) through a Mobile Application. A Smart Phone Is required.

    When Do I get paid?

    You get paid every 2 weeks on Friday (2 weeks in arrears). The money gets directly deposited from the DHT Bank to your bank.

     What will I do?

    As a Harvest Crew Member you will either drive a Combine, Truck or Tractor. If you have mechanical skills, you could work on repair and maintenance of vehicles and machinery.

     What type of training will I receive?

    All Crew Members receive Company, Safety and Commercial Truck Driving (CDL) Training.

  • Company Training includes a 2-week orientation where you will be required to apply for a Social Security Card, take a Physical, Drug Testing, Mobile App and Web Portal Training, Etc.
  • Safety Training will be provided through In-House classroom training sessions.
  • CDL Training will be provide by a State Certified Training Instructor.

    • Where will I work?

    Your training will be in Arizona until the Harvest Season begins. In the past, DHT has had jobs in Arizona, Mississippi, South Dakota, New Mexico, California, North Dakota, and many more states. You will be assigned to a crew that travels to one or more of these states.

    What type of lodging will I be staying at?

    For the most part the lodging is very modest, and you will be staying in a Mobile Home. Once the crews leave Arizona for other locations you will be staying in a Travel Trailer with sleeping accommodations, Kitchen Facilities, TV, Air Conditioning, etc. NOTE: ALL EMPLOYEES ARE REQUIRED TO KEEP THEIR LODGING CLEAN OR THEY WILL BE CHARGED A FEE FOR CLEANING.

    Will I get any time off?

    Before the Harvest Season begins you may have some weekends off. However, once the Harvest season begins, unless it rains or we’re waiting for crops to mature, there will not be any time off.

    Are there any other benefits that I should be aware of?

  • DHT provides its employees with the training necessary to test for a CDL License. State Certified Trainers are provided along with the trucks used for training. Acquiring a CDL License is a valuable skill and will aide in your future employment.
  • Employees have electronic access (through web portal and mobile application) to all onboarding documents they have submitted plus company produced paperwork including payroll and daily work records.
  • Health Insurance is not provided but DHT has workers compensation insurance which will covers any accidental injuries that might occur during work hours.
  • DHT may provide its employees access to courtesy vehicles when prior approval is granted.

    • Is there opportunity for career advancement?

    Yes, you be trained in using different types of equipment and learn many valuable skills that will assist you in your future career placements. Plus, many DHT Employees return from year to year, and help provide company leadership. In addition, most times they will receive an increase in pay.

    What countries do other employees come from?

    In the past, we have hired employees from many different countries, but the primary locations are Ireland, New Zealand, Mexico, Brazil, United Kingdom, and Australia.

     When and how should I apply for a Job?

    You can apply at the DHT Website anytime, but the best time to apply is in January or February each year.
